I am a Lecturer in the Department of Political Science at Rice University.

I use formal and computational models to unpack how contentious politics works. My research examines protest, repression, concessions, and how the choices of activists, governments, and the public shape one another. You can read more about my [research]({{< ref "/research/" >}}).

In my teaching, I present political science as a set of tools for understanding real-world problems. My courses ask students to build fluency with those tools and apply them to real cases, rather than simply read about them. You can read more about my [teaching]({{< ref "/teaching/" >}}).

I am an alumnus of the [Empirical Implications of Theoretical Models Institute](https://eitminstitute.org/) and a former [United States Institute of Peace Peace Scholar](https://www.usip.org/grants-fellowships/fellowships/peace-scholar-fellowship-program).
